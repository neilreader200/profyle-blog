Professional Headshots in Manchester

Location guide

Last updated 30 April 2026. By Sarah Whitfield.

Manchester occupies a peculiar position in UK professional culture. The city has the depth of professional services that you'd expect of a regional capital — Spinningfields holds 1,500-lawyer offices and Big Four UK regional HQs — but it also has a media presence at MediaCityUK that no other UK city outside London can claim, and a tech sector centred on the Northern Quarter and the Oxford Road corridor that's been growing for nearly a decade. The professional headshot you need for a Manchester audience reflects those distinct sectors, and they don't all want the same kind of photo.

This is a guide to getting a professional headshot if you're Manchester-based, focused on what each of the city's main sectors actually expects — and where Profyle fits in.

Manchester's professional sectors

Media (Salford / MediaCityUK)

BBC, ITV Granada, Dock10. Producers, journalists, on-screen contributors. Need approachable, slightly warmer photos than the City equivalent.

Legal (Spinningfields)

DLA Piper, Eversheds, Pinsent Masons, Addleshaw Goddard. Partner-track and senior associate photos. Closer to the London legal aesthetic but a touch less starched.

Accountancy & advisory

Big Four regional HQs, BDO, Grant Thornton, plus a deep mid-tier. ICAEW directory profiles, firm websites, partner announcements.

Tech and digital

Northern Quarter founders, AutoTrader, On The Beach, Boohoo, the BBC's R&D arm. Less suit-and-tie than London tech, often jeans and dark T-shirts.

Universities and research

UoM, MMU, Salford. Department pages, ResearchGate, ORCID, conference programme bylines. Different from US faculty photo conventions.

NHS Greater Manchester

MFT (Manchester University NHS Foundation Trust), Salford Royal, Christie. Trust directories, royal college profiles, registrar-level academic listings.

Patterns we see in Manchester orders

The MediaCityUK update. Producers, presenters, and journalists refreshing their headshots ahead of a new commission. The output needs to look broadcast-friendly — composed but warm, not corporate-stiff. Standard or Professional tier with multiple looks for different programme contexts.

The Spinningfields partner promotion. Newly-promoted partners and senior associates needing photos for their firm's website, Chambers profile (where applicable), and the inevitable LinkedIn announcement. Professional tier — varied outfits to support different uses, Likeness Guarantee included.

The tech founder press round. A funded Northern Quarter startup announcing a Series A. Needs the founder photo for press release, investor portfolio pages, and a Sifted profile. Premium tier with priority delivery if the cycle is tight.

The accountancy partnership announcement. Mid-tier firm promoting partners. Often happening in batches — Profyle's per-person tiers work better than coordinating a studio day across busy partners.

The university department page refresh. Whole-department photo updates that historically needed a coordinated studio day. Each academic uploads selfies separately, gets a consistent gallery back, picks their favourite. No scheduling of multiple busy academics into the same room.

Where a Manchester photographer is the right call

Manchester has good portrait photographers and there are circumstances where they earn the spend. A Manchester Evening News executive feature, a published book sleeve, a national magazine appearance, the Northern Power 50 list — these are the situations where the marginal polish of a studio session is worth £200–£400. For day-to-day LinkedIn use, a partner page, a Chambers entry, or a department directory, AI headshots are well within tolerance and a fraction of the cost.
